--- layout: layouts/base.njk title: Print Media description: Guidelines for print applications breadcrumbs: - label: Home url: / - label: Application url: /application - label: Print Media url: /application/print-media category: Application --- ## Print Media Guidelines for applying brand elements in print materials. ## Print Best Practices 1. Use high-resolution images (300 DPI minimum) 2. Use CMYK color mode for print 3. Include bleed areas when needed 4. Use appropriate paper stocks 5. Follow printer specifications ## Business Cards Guidelines for designing business cards that represent our brand professionally. ### Design Specifications #### Standard Size - **US Standard**: 3.5" × 2" (89mm × 51mm) - **European Standard**: 85mm × 55mm - Include 0.125" bleed area #### Layout - Place logo prominently - Include essential contact information - Maintain clear space - Use brand colors appropriately ### Required Elements - Logo (primary or secondary variation) - Company name - Individual name and title - Contact information (phone, email, website) - Physical address (if applicable) ### Optional Elements - Social media handles - QR codes - Tagline or value proposition - Additional contact methods ### Design Guidelines #### Typography - Use brand typefaces - Ensure readability at small sizes - Maintain hierarchy - Use appropriate font sizes #### Colors - Use brand colors - Ensure sufficient contrast - Consider print limitations - Test color accuracy #### Paper and Finish - Choose appropriate paper stock - Consider finish options (matte, gloss, textured) - Align with brand personality - Test print quality ### Best Practices 1. Keep design clean and uncluttered 2. Ensure all text is readable 3. Maintain brand consistency 4. Use high-quality printing 5. Proof carefully before printing ## Brochures Guidelines for designing brochures that effectively communicate our brand and message. ### Brochure Types #### Tri-Fold - Standard 8.5" × 11" (folded) - Three panels for content - Front and back covers #### Bi-Fold - Standard 8.5" × 11" (folded) - Two panels for content - Front and back covers #### Single Sheet - Various sizes - One or two-sided - Simple format ### Design Guidelines #### Layout - Clear visual hierarchy - Organized information flow - Appropriate use of white space - Consistent branding #### Content - Compelling headlines - Clear, concise copy - Relevant imagery - Strong calls-to-action #### Brand Elements - Prominent logo placement - Brand colors throughout - Brand typography - Consistent visual style ### Required Elements - Logo - Company information - Key messaging - Contact information - Call-to-action ### Best Practices 1. Design with the fold in mind 2. Use high-quality images (300 DPI) 3. Maintain brand consistency 4. Ensure readability 5. Include clear calls-to-action 6. Test print quality ## Packaging Guidelines for designing packaging that reflects our brand and creates a memorable unboxing experience. ### Packaging Principles #### Brand Identity - Prominent brand elements - Consistent visual style - Brand colors and typography - Memorable design #### Functionality - Clear product information - Easy to open and use - Protective and durable - Appropriate sizing #### Sustainability - Consider environmental impact - Use sustainable materials when possible - Minimize waste - Communicate sustainability efforts ### Design Elements #### Logo Placement - Visible and prominent - Appropriate size for package - Maintain clear space - Use correct logo variation #### Color and Typography - Use brand colors - Apply brand typography - Ensure readability - Create visual interest #### Imagery - High-quality product images - Brand-aligned photography - Appropriate use of graphics - Clear product representation ### Required Information - Product name and description - Brand logo and name - Legal requirements (if applicable) - Contact information - Barcode/UPC (if applicable) ### Best Practices 1. Design for the unboxing experience 2. Maintain brand consistency 3. Ensure all information is clear 4. Test packaging functionality 5. Consider production constraints 6. Balance design with cost
