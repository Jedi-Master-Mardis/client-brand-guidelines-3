Our colors convey trust, professionalism, and service excellence across all applications - from vehicle graphics and uniforms to marketing materials and digital platforms. ## Color Principles 1. Use primary colors for main brand elements including logo, key CTAs, and vehicle graphics 2. Use secondary colors to support and complement, providing visual hierarchy 3. Maintain proper contrast for accessibility, especially important for safety signage and vehicle visibility 4. Follow color usage guidelines for each application (print, digital, vehicle graphics, uniforms) 5. Ensure colors remain consistent across all locations and service types 6. Consider visibility and safety requirements for outdoor applications, especially on fuel trucks and emergency response vehicles ## Primary Colors Primary colors are the foundation of our brand identity and should be used for key brand elements. ### Primary Color Palette

### Usage Guidelines - Use primary colors for logos, key CTAs, and important brand elements - Apply primary colors prominently on service vehicles for brand recognition - Maintain sufficient contrast for text readability, especially on vehicle graphics and outdoor signage - Use color variations (lighter/darker) for hierarchy and depth in marketing materials - Avoid overuse - let primary colors have impact and maintain professional appearance - Ensure colors meet safety and visibility standards for fleet vehicles ### Color Variations Each primary color includes a full range of tints and shades for various applications. ## Secondary Colors Secondary colors support and complement the primary palette, providing flexibility and depth to our designs. ### Secondary Color Palette
